Frequently Asked Questions

    + What is Track'd?
    Track'd is a responsive website that helps UW students take better advantage of campus events and resources. We empower RSOs and other groups on campus to share information about their events, in addition to grabbing data from places like public Facebook events and the main UW calendar.

    + How did you come up with the idea for this application?
    As UW students, we're often on campus and want to see if there are any fun events happening during the times we're free. However, we quickly found that it was really hard to get a comprehensive list of events happening on campus at any given time. Thus, Track'd was born!

    + What does your app do that doesn't already exist?
    Our main goals were to aggregate existing data, all the while providing an intuitive UI for users to explore. We were unable to find existing applications that meet both of these two standards, which is what motivated us to develop our own.

    + How do you control the information going onto your site?
    For now, we're not censoring, because all students need to log on with their UW NetID first, which is in itself a form of moderation. However, we're working to implement a reporting system which will notify a moderator about an inappropriate posting.

    + Why don't you have people log in with Facebook?
    We wanted to promote exploration within our app -- find events outside of your normal interests, meet people you don't currently know. Consequently, Facebook didn't seem like the right platform to inspire that sort of discovery. Additionally, since this is an application for UW students, it seemed like a better idea to let students log in with their UW NetID, because whereas some UW students may not have a Facebook, all students and faculty have NetIDs.

    + Who created Track'd?
    We are a team of five informatics students from the UW iSchool. Check out our About page for more information about us, or email trackdapp@gmail.com to reach us directly.
